Intro

Hello there, and thanks for taking the time to learn more about both my professional, and personal life. Professionally my career history includes experience in fields such as: working in the bar industry throughout school, an It support role that includes some network administartion related duties, and most recelently, web development.

Personally, I don't really possess any distinguishing talents to maybe brand myself like some may have; like being a musician, nor do I have any worldy life altering experiences that have helped shaped who I am. Although, I was in a band for a week in high school, and we played a sold out show of like SIX people in some backyard. In my head it was like we were playing a sold out show at the Garden and we were the beatles, and that's the way I am going to remember it. So yeah, maybe I am a musician then.

One thing I do have in my life though; is an amazing and beautiful 20 month old daughter. I am a very doting father, and I'll dote on my daughter all day. No shame. I am a single father, and I have been taking care of her on my own since she was born. It can be alot of responsibility sometimes, but I love being a dad, and she is the best thing that has ever happened to me. She is also the most important person in my life which is why she is really the driving force behind me wanting to progress even further in my professional career. That way I can support her and provide her with a quality life that she deserves, and I also possess personal expectations as a father as to what as far as her upbringing. I spend the pretty much all of my time on either work or dev stuff, and hanging out with my little lady. That's not to say I don't occaisonally have a little time to spare to do other things as I do have some hobbies actually, and that mostly inludes playing video games. To be specific I love anything Nintendo really, and JRPGs. I am also a big sports fan; I love the NBA. I go on reddit not Instagram and I don't like avacados. It's a hot take but I think they suck. That's about it really.
